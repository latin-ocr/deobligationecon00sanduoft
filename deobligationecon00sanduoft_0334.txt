The two Conditions are,

First, That nothing be done to the prejudice of the Prince, the

preservation of whose person and dignity is essential to the Public
Safety.

Second, That nothing be 11066 against established Laws and Rights,
except with the consent of the Prince, express or presumed. And the
consent of the Prince may, in his absence, be reasonably presumed,
then, when there is the presence of so great and evident a necessity,
that no sane man can doubt that the Prince, if he were present,

would be willing to relax the Law.
