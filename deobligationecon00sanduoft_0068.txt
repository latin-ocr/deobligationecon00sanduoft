And thus these three conditions being required for the goodness of
an Act,—lawful Matter, right Intention, and due circumstances 3 it is
plain that right Intention of itself does not suffice: and nothing can
be done with a good Conscience, with whatever Intention, which is, by
reason of the Matter, unlawful, or vitiated by the Circumstances.
