The whole texture of the paragraph shews not a trace of any
authority granted to the subjects, of acting against Law, or judging of
the safety of the People in opposition to the Prince. The Suprema
Lex, superior to every actual Law, is put in the hands of him who has
the Reyium Imperium and Militioe Jus Summum.

And that you may have the consent of the greatest writer in each
language on this subJect, Aristotle says: “The Ruler, whether he be
one or many, must judge on what subjects the Laws are incompetent
to pronounce in particular, on account of the difficulty of including

every case in general expressions.’'
