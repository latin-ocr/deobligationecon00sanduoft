5. It is true that Conscii in Latin is, as our author says, applied to
persons who have a common knowledge of a design or event3 but in
the sense of Conscientia which we consider in this work, the knowledge
appears to be conceived as common to the man in his two capacities,
the man known, and the same man knowing. The author's notion
that Conscience implies a knowledge which we have in common with
God, is rather fanciful than solid.
