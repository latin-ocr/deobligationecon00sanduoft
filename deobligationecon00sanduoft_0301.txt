11. We say, Thirdly, that even if the Law be not only made with
an evil intention, but be in some measure a mischievous law, still
the subject is bound to obey the law, when made by due authority,
provided the matter of the law, the thing commanded, be such as can
be done without sin. The Reason is this: that each person ought to
take especial care of that which belongs to his own business3 and not
to be too anxious and scrupulous in matters which are the business of
others. “Every man shall bear his own burthen,” Gal. vi. 5. If the
Legislator is wanting in his duty, what is that to thee? Take that
thine is, and go thy way. As to his intention, whether it is right or
otherwise, let him look to that3 he will have to render an account to
his Lord, of his actions and of his intentions too. And thou, if
thou obey not, wilt have to render to the same Lord an account of thy
disobedience.
