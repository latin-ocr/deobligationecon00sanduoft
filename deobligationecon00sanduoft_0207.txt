14. Fourth Question. What Law is to be deemed so unjust that
not only it does not oblige, but that it is not allowable to obey it?

We answer by explaining the kinds of InJustice which may affect
Laws.

1. A Law may be unjust on the part of the Efficient Cause, the
maker3 being made by one who has no due authority. Here is a
defect of Commutative Justice, because the Maker of the Law assumes
more than belongs to him. Such a Law does not oblige3 but the
subject, if he ple-ase, may waive his right and obey.
