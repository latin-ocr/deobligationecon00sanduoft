’ This is a dangerous errour, and rests upon two false assumptions:
that Princes have received their power from the People3 and that he
who concedes power to another may revoke it.

If the latter assumption were true, how idle were all Samuel's
warnings to the Jews, respecting the evil which would arise from their
having a king. They might have said, “If we find it so great an evil,
we will revoke the power which we have given.'’ But it is plain that
such a principle was not then acknowledged.
