or obJect), if it be commanded by Law, from being allowable, becomes
necessary to right acti0n3 if it be forbidden by Law, from being
open, becomes wrong.

This does not happen from any change in the thing itself, either
physical or ethica13 but from the Obligation which the law super-
induces. Thus it comes to pass that what was,‘as by nature and of
itself, so in practice and with regard to us, open either way3 when the
authority of the law supervenes, becomes in practice and with
regard to us, no ‘longer open, but according to the law, either wrong, or
necessary to right action.
