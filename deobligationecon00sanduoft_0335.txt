The Public Safety is the End, Laws are the Means3 and the
Means must be subservient to the End. And the Prince, who is the
principal agent in the making of Laws, must be presumed to think so,
and therefore tacitly to consent to whatever is, without his being con-
sulted, done for the common safety under manifest necessity.
