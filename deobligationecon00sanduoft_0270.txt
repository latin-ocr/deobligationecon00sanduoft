. from blame on account of his invincible ignorance, and therefore

The same is true of many other kinds of Laws3 as those which
divest persons of rights, recall privileges, regulate interest of money,
fix the prices of commodities3 treated of at length by Canonists and
Divines.

But though this damage be called penalty or punishment, it Is very
improperly so called. Punishment inflicted on an innocent person is
a wrong3 but damage without wrong, damnum sine injuria, may fall
upon an innocent person.
