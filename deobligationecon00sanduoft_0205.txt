And hence the answer to the argument, that obedience is not
disputatious. The subject is to submit himself to the supreme
authority without disputing it, but not to any subordinate autho-

rity, when contrary to the supreme. And, thus, we must obey God
always.

So Abraham obeyed the command of God, and did what, not so
commanded, would have been wrong and wicked. But the commands
of men are to be examined. 1 Thess. v. 21, applies to commands as
well as doctrines. Hence we reject absolutely authority, such as
papists ascribe to the Pope, and religious orders to their Heads.
