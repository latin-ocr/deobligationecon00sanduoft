The reason of this difference is, that that antecedent necessity, which
the Law presupposes, requires an assent of the Judgment3 but the
consequent necessity, which the law creates, requires only the consent
of the Will, to do the prescribed external act. Now an act of the Will
cannot interfere with Liberty of Conscience, but an act of the J udg-
