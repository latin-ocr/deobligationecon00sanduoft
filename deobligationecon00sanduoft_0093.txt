9. But secondly: the actions which are praised in Scripture and
proposed for imitation, are not necessarily to be imitated in the
aggregate: we must distinguish, and imitate them only as to that part
which is praised. For God sometimes in regarding a mixed action,
praises the good part, and passes over the bad. As when the Zorct
praised the unjust steward, Luke xvi. 8 (namely his lord, not the Lord
God), his prudence is praised, not his injustice. And when the
Egyptian midwives (Exod. i. 19) gave a false account to the king, and
God dealt well with them, it was their humanity and pIety which was
acceptable, not their falsehood.
